What can You visit in Belgrade?

Explore The Old Kalemegdan Fortress

Exploring the historic Kalemegdan Fortress is a journey through time and a must-visit destination in Belgrade. This iconic fortress, strategically located at the confluence of the Sava and Danube rivers, has witnessed centuries of history. As you wander through its ancient walls and ramparts, you'll be transported back to a bygone era. The fortress not only offers a glimpse into Belgrade's rich past but also presents breathtaking panoramic views of the city, making it a perfect spot to watch the sunset over Belgrade. Kalemegdan Fortress stands as a testament to the city's resilience and the beauty of its heritage, making it a memorable destination for history enthusiasts and travelers of all kinds.

Skadarlija

Skadarlija, often lovingly referred to as Belgrade's bohemian quarter, is a true gem nestled in the heart of the city. This charming cobbled street is a portal to the 19th century, where time seems to stand still. As you meander through Skadarlija's picturesque lanes, you'll be captivated by its unique atmosphere. The street is renowned for its quaint restaurants and cafes, each offering a taste of Serbia's rich culinary heritage. From the delightful aroma of traditional Serbian dishes to the soul-stirring sounds of live music, Skadarlija immerses you in a warm and friendly ambiance. It's a place where you can savor the past, enjoy the present, and create unforgettable memories, all within the enchanting embrace of Belgrade's bohemian quarter

St. Sava Temple

The St. Sava Temple is a testament to both Serbia's rich spiritual heritage and its architectural magnificence. As one of the largest Orthodox churches in the world, it stands as a symbol of faith and grandeur in the heart of Belgrade. The temple's impressive size and intricate details make it a must-visit attraction for anyone exploring the city. Whether you are a religious pilgrim or an admirer of architectural marvels, the beauty of the St. Sava Temple is undeniable. The interior, adorned with ornate frescoes and a serene atmosphere, provides a peaceful refuge for contemplation. Its grand, white marble façade and resplendent domes are a striking sight, and the temple's significance extends beyond religious bounds, making it a cultural and architectural treasure of Belgrade.

Museum of Contemporary Art

The Museum of Contemporary Art in Belgrade is a cultural gem that beckons art enthusiasts and curious minds alike. This institution stands as a testament to the thriving contemporary art scene in Serbia. Inside its walls, you'll discover a captivating collection of modern and contemporary art, showcasing the creativity of both Serbian and international artists. The museum's architecture itself is a work of art, and its galleries are a canvas for the expressions of innovation and imagination. With every brushstroke, sculpture, and installation, the museum offers visitors a journey through the evolving landscapes of human creativity. From thought-provoking exhibitions to visually stimulating installations, the Museum of Contemporary Art is a place where art takes center stage, pushing the boundaries of imagination and offering a delightful sensory experience for all who enter.

Taste Serbian Cuisine

Tasting Serbian cuisine is a delectable journey for your taste buds and a delightful exploration of the country's culinary heritage. Belgrade, often considered a food lover's paradise, offers an array of delectable dishes and flavors that will leave a lasting impression. You can't visit Serbia without savoring local specialties like 'ćevapi,' grilled minced meat, and 'sarma,' cabbage rolls bursting with flavor. Don't forget to try 'ajvar,' a pepper-based condiment that adds a unique zing to dishes. The local markets, such as Kalenić Market, beckon you to sample fresh produce and homemade delicacies, immersing you in the vibrant food culture. Whether you opt for a cozy restaurant, a bustling street vendor, or a local market stall, Serbian cuisine promises an authentic and mouthwatering experience, making it an essential part of your visit to Belgrade.
