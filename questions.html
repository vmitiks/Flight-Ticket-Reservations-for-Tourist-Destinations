The ticket didn't arrive

1. Contact the Airline: The first step is to get in touch with the airline's customer service or support. They should provide a clear and detailed explanation of the situation, including the booking reference or ticket number.
2. Check Spam or Junk Folder: Sometimes, email confirmations and e-tickets may land in the recipient's spam or junk folder. It's a good practice to check these folders to ensure the ticket email wasn't misplaced.
3. Request Resending: If the ticket email was indeed not received, the customer can request the airline to resend the ticket and related details. Ensure they provide the correct email address for the resend.
4. Verify Payment: Make sure that the payment for the ticket was successfully processed. If there were any issues with payment, it could be the reason for not receiving the ticket.

Ticket return to exchange

1. Check Ticket Rules: Review the terms and conditions of the airline ticket. Understand the rules regarding refunds, exchanges, and cancellations. Different types of tickets (e.g., non-refundable, flexible) may have different policies.
2. Contact the Airline: Get in touch with the airline's customer service or support. It's essential to communicate your intention to return or exchange the ticket. Provide all relevant details, including the booking reference or ticket number.
3. Timing Matters: Act promptly. Many airlines have specific timeframes during which ticket exchanges or refunds can be processed. Be aware of any deadlines for making changes.
4. Penalties and Fees: Understand the fees and penalties associated with ticket returns or exchanges. Airlines may charge a fee for processing these requests, and the refund amount may be reduced accordingly.

Changing passport details

1. Contact the Airline: Reach out to the airline's customer service or support as soon as you realize the need to change your passport details. Inform them of the required changes and provide any necessary information.
2. Provide Documentation: Airlines typically require official documentation to process passport changes. Be prepared to provide a copy of your updated passport or any other identification documents requested by the airline.
3. Timing Matters: Act promptly, especially if you have an upcoming flight. Passport changes may take time, so it's essential to ensure that the updated information is reflected in your booking before your travel date.
4. Be Prepared to Pay: Airlines may charge a fee for processing passport changes. Be aware of any associated costs and make payment arrangements as needed.

About luggage

Transportation of pets

1. Pet Policy Overview: Start by giving an overview of the airline's pet policy. Explain whether pets are allowed on board, including in the cabin or as checked baggage, and if there are any restrictions based on the pet's size and breed.
2. Types of Pets Allowed: Clarify which types of pets are accepted, including dogs, cats, birds, and other small animals. Specify any restrictions on exotic or uncommon pets.
3. Pet Carrier Requirements: Outline the airline's requirements for pet carriers, including size, materials, and ventilation. Provide information on where passengers can purchase or rent suitable carriers.
